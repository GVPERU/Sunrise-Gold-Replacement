Stage 2 | Replacement and Field Redesign

Sunrise to Gold Replacement Strategy

Descriptive analysis of the decision to replace Sunrise with Gold and the preferred implementation model: higher density in soil versus a container-based system.

Context Decision Alternatives Financial Stage 2 Impact Proposal Conclusion

1. Context

Stage 2 is currently planted with Sunrise. The replacement strategy must address two different questions: first, whether Sunrise should be replaced by Gold; second, how Gold should be implemented.

Key Question

Plant Performance

Is current plant productivity competitive enough to justify maintaining the existing Sunrise structure?

Key Question

Productivity per Hectare

Are we maximizing yield per hectare under the current planting design and varietal mix?

Key Question

Production System

Should we migrate to a container-based model, or can we capture the required gains in soil?

Key Question

Optimal Density

What density maximizes performance while preserving operational simplicity and financial discipline?

Objective: evaluate the most operationally viable approach to maximize productivity per hectare in Stage 2, currently 100% Sunrise, proposed conversion to Gold.

2. The Core Decision

The replacement of Sunrise with Gold is not the main point of debate. The analysis shows that Gold is superior from a quality, efficiency, contribution and strategic relevance perspective.

Decision Already Supported

  • Gold generates higher contribution than Sunrise.
  • Gold has a higher average price due to a stronger jumbo profile.
  • Gold improves harvest productivity and cost per kg.
  • Gold is better aligned with our strategic focus on quality and efficiency.

Actual Discussion

  • How should Gold be implemented?
  • Should we increase density in soil using the current system?
  • Or should we adopt a container-based model with materially higher CAPEX?

3. Strategic Focus

Our strategic priorities are efficiency and quality. The implementation decision should be evaluated based on how each alternative contributes to these priorities, as well as to our relevance within the group.

Efficiency

  • Higher harvest productivity.
  • Lower cost per kg.
  • Better worker earning potential.
  • More attractive farm environment for labor.

Quality

  • Higher firmness.
  • Larger fruit size.
  • More consistent postharvest performance.
  • Lower operational risk than Sunrise.

Relevance

  • Higher contribution to the group’s value mix.
  • Greater participation in the most valuable genetics.
  • Stronger role as a relevant production platform.

4. Implementation Alternatives

The analysis considers three reference scenarios. The main comparison is between increasing density in soil and moving into containers at high density.

Scenario Density Yield Curve CAPEX / ha Comment
Base Gold Scenario 4,915 plants/ha Longer ~USD 23,000 Current design adapted to Gold.
Higher Density in Soil 6,265 plants/ha Shorter / lower 2026 kg gap ~USD 27,600 +27% density with a known operating system.
Higher Density in Containers 6,265 plants/ha Shorter ~USD 105,000 Higher density but with much higher CAPEX and operational complexity.

The container alternative requires almost four times the CAPEX per hectare versus the higher-density soil option. Therefore, any additional yield benefit would need to be large and sufficiently validated to justify the capital intensity and operational change.

5. Operational Considerations

Standardization

Changing the layout and production system affects operational standardization. Soil-based higher density preserves the current model and avoids introducing a different operating structure within the same field.

Sanitary Risk

Higher density shortens the yield curve but may introduce additional sanitary complexity. This must be managed, particularly if the system departs from the current validated field operation.

Varietal Factor

The varietal factor is highly relevant. The feasibility of increasing density has been analyzed specifically with Eureka Gold, which is the variety that enables this change in soil.

Execution Timing

Planting date compliance is critical. The proposed soil scenario represents lower CAPEX and lower risk, with faster and more immediate execution.

8. Proposal

Replace Sunrise with Gold in Stage 2 by increasing planting density in soil.

Why Soil?

  • Maintains the current operating model.
  • Requires materially lower CAPEX than containers.
  • Reduces implementation risk.
  • Allows faster execution.

Why Higher Density?

  • Increases productivity per hectare.
  • Shortens the yield recovery curve.
  • Better captures the productive potential of Gold.
  • Improves contribution without changing the production system.

Why Not Containers?

  • ~USD 105,000 CAPEX/ha.
  • Almost 4x the soil high-density CAPEX.
  • Higher operational complexity.
  • Requires stronger validation to justify the incremental investment.

9. Conclusions

1. Gold is the Correct Varietal Move

Replacing Sunrise with Gold improves quality, efficiency, contribution and strategic relevance.

2. Implementation Matters

The central question is not whether to move to Gold, but how to do it with the best balance of return, speed, risk and capital discipline.

3. Soil Higher Density is Preferred

Increasing density in soil captures the value of Gold while preserving the current system and avoiding the much higher CAPEX of containers.

The most economically and operationally convenient alternative is to replace Sunrise with Gold in Stage 2, increasing density to 6,265 plants per hectare in soil.

This allows us to reach higher yields than the current structure, with approximately one quarter of the CAPEX required by the container alternative, while preserving operational simplicity and improving contribution.
