Recommendation for FTF discussion and alignment 08
Implementation logic

Execution should be managed as a controlled renewal program, not as an isolated planting change.

How we execute
1
Final alignment
Confirm FTF agreement on scenario, density, variety and business rationale.
2
Planting schedule
Lock the critical planting window and backward-plan operational requirements.
3
Field preparation
Prepare soil, irrigation, layout and operating standards for the higher-density design.
4
Controlled rollout
Execute renewal gradually, controlling yield gap, labor needs and field standardization.
5
Monitoring
Track plant performance, productivity per hectare, sanitary pressure and economic KPIs.

Critical success factor

Planting date compliance is the main execution condition. Delays would reduce the value of the shorter yield curve and increase the 2026 productivity gap.

Management principle

The higher-density system must preserve operational standardization. Any design change should be simple enough to scale and manage consistently.

Implementation discipline protects the economics of the decision 09
Risk management

The recommendation reduces capital risk, but still requires active control of technical and operational variables.

Main risks and mitigants

Risk 1: planting delay

✓

Backward-plan from planting deadline, with clear accountability for plant availability, land preparation and irrigation readiness.

Risk 2: higher sanitary pressure

✓

Use a preventive sanitary matrix adapted to higher density, phenological stage and seasonal risk probability.

Risk 3: unvalidated upside per plant

✓

Do not underwrite unproven upside as guaranteed. Treat additional plant-level potential as upside optionality.

Risk 4: operational complexity

✓

Keep the system in soil to avoid unnecessary complexity, reduce CAPEX, and preserve consistency across the operating platform.

The preferred scenario is lower-risk than pots, not risk-free 10
