Stage 2 Replacement Strategy: Sunrise to Gold

A descriptive analysis of why replacing Sunrise with Gold is the right strategic decision, and why increasing density in soil is the preferred implementation approach.

1. Strategic Focus

Our strategic priorities are efficiency and quality. The replacement strategy must therefore be evaluated not only financially, but also through its impact on productivity, fruit quality, operational simplicity and relevance within the group.

Efficiency

Gold significantly improves harvesting productivity, reduces cost per kg and improves worker earning potential.

Quality

Gold offers better firmness, larger fruit size, more consistency and stronger postharvest performance than Sunrise.

Relevance

Maximizing Gold strengthens our contribution to the highest-value genetic mix within the group.

2. Core Decision

The replacement of Sunrise with Gold is not the main debate. Based on quality, efficiency, contribution and strategic relevance, the migration to Gold is clearly superior.

The real decision is how to implement Gold: through increased density in soil, using the current operating system, or through a higher-CAPEX container-based model.

6. Operational View

Soil Higher Density

Uses the current production system, requires materially lower CAPEX, allows faster execution and avoids introducing a new operating model.

Containers

Requires almost 4x the investment per hectare, adds operational complexity and changes the standardization of the current production model.

7. Conclusion

Replacing Sunrise with Gold is the value-creating decision. The key question is implementation.

Increasing density in soil captures the strategic benefits of Gold with significantly lower CAPEX, lower operational complexity, faster implementation and stronger alignment with the current field model.

Therefore, the preferred alternative is to replace Sunrise with Gold in Stage 2 using higher density in soil.
