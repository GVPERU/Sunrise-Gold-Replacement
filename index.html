Family Farms Peru

Stage 2 Replacement Strategy

Descriptive analysis of the Sunrise to Gold replacement decision and the preferred implementation model for Stage 2.

Context Framework Gold Financial Implementation Proposal
01 | Context

What is being evaluated

Stage 2 is currently planted with Sunrise. The analysis must separate two decisions: whether Sunrise should be replaced by Gold, and how Gold should be implemented.

First decision: Sunrise vs Gold

The replacement of Sunrise with Gold is strongly supported by the analysis. Gold improves quality, efficiency, contribution and strategic relevance.

Second decision: implementation

The main discussion is whether Gold should be implemented through higher density in soil, using the current operating system, or through a container-based model.

Objective: evaluate the most operationally viable approach to maximize productivity, quality, contribution and relevance in Stage 2.
02 | Strategic Framework

Decision criteria

The decision should not be evaluated only as a financial exercise. It must be consistent with the strategic focus of Family Farms Peru: efficiency, quality and relevance.

Efficiency

  • Maximize productivity per hectare.
  • Improve harvest productivity.
  • Reduce cost per kilogram.
  • Improve worker earning potential.

Quality

  • Improve fruit quality at origin.
  • Increase premium and jumbo fruit share.
  • Improve firmness and postharvest performance.
  • Reduce variability and quality risk.

Relevance

  • Maintain a relevant position within the group.
  • Increase participation in the highest-value varietal mix.
  • Concentrate acreage in the best available genetics.
03 | Sunrise to Gold

Gold is the correct varietal move

The change from Sunrise to Gold is not the main debate. Gold is superior from a quality, efficiency, contribution and strategic relevance perspective.

Quality

  • Higher firmness.
  • Larger fruit size.
  • Higher jumbo percentage.
  • More consistent postharvest performance.

Efficiency

  • Higher harvest productivity.
  • Lower cost per kilogram.
  • Better labor economics.
  • More predictable field performance.

Relevance

  • Gold is currently the highest-value variety in the portfolio.
  • Increasing Gold acreage strengthens contribution to the group.
  • The objective is relevance, not exclusivity.
Conclusion: replacing Sunrise with Gold is the right direction. The remaining question is how to implement Gold.

06 | Implementation Alternatives

How should Gold be implemented?

Once Gold is selected, the main discussion is implementation. The alternatives are higher density in soil or higher density in containers.

Strategy Density Yield Curve CAPEX / ha Operational View
Base Gold Scenario 4,915 plants/ha Longer ~USD 23,000 Current design adapted to Gold
Higher Density in Soil 6,265 plants/ha Shorter / lower kg gap ~USD 27,600 Preferred
Higher Density in Containers 6,265 plants/ha Shorter ~USD 105,000 High CAPEX
Containers require almost four times the investment per hectare. Any incremental yield benefit must be large, validated and sufficient to compensate for the additional capital and operational complexity.
07 | Soil vs Containers

Certainty versus potential

The main advantage of containers is potential. The main advantage of higher density in soil is certainty.

08 | Known and Unvalidated Factors

What should drive the decision

What we know

  • Gold performs better than Sunrise.
  • Gold supports increased density in soil.
  • The soil-based model is already operationally validated.
  • The main value creation comes from the varietal replacement itself.
  • The soil alternative has materially lower CAPEX.

What is not yet validated

  • The exact incremental yield advantage of containers.
  • Whether the container benefit compensates almost 4x CAPEX.
  • Whether additional complexity affects execution, timing or standardization.
  • Whether container-based performance is sufficiently validated under our conditions.
09 | Proposal

Recommended implementation

Replace Sunrise with Gold in Stage 2, increasing density to 6,265 plants per hectare in soil.

Captures Gold value

Delivers the strategic, quality and contribution benefits of the varietal change.

Preserves operating model

Maintains the current system and avoids unnecessary operational disruption.

Improves capital discipline

Requires approximately one quarter of the CAPEX versus containers.

10 | Conclusions

Final conclusion

1. Sunrise to Gold is the correct move

It improves quality, efficiency, contribution and relevance. The decision is consistent with the strategic priorities of Family Farms Peru.

2. Higher density in soil is the preferred implementation

It captures the majority of expected benefits with lower CAPEX, lower complexity, faster execution and a validated operating model.

The most economically and operationally attractive alternative is to replace Sunrise with Gold in Stage 2 while increasing density to 6,265 plants per hectare in soil.
