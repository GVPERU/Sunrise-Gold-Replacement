01 | Context

What are we solving?

The analysis has two separate layers. First, whether Sunrise should be replaced by Gold. Second, once Gold is selected, how it should be implemented: higher density in soil or a container-based model.

Current Situation

Stage 2 is currently 100% Sunrise

  • Current varietal structure is becoming less competitive.
  • Sunrise is more demanding from a quality and efficiency standpoint.
  • The opportunity is to replace the block with Gold and improve the value mix.
Decision Logic

The key question is implementation

  • The Sunrise to Gold decision is strongly supported by the analysis.
  • The main discussion is how to implement Gold.
  • Family Tree prefers containers; we believe soil with higher density is the better alternative today.
Objective: evaluate the most operationally viable approach to maximize productivity, quality, contribution and relevance in Stage 2.
02 | Strategic Framework

Decision criteria: efficiency, quality and relevance

The decision should not be analyzed only as a financial exercise. It must be tested against the strategic focus of Family Farms Perú.

Focus 1

Efficiency

  • Maximize productivity per hectare.
  • Maximize harvest productivity.
  • Reduce cost per kg.
  • Improve worker earning potential.
Focus 2

Quality

  • Maximize quality at origin.
  • Increase premium fruit share.
  • Improve firmness and condition.
  • Reduce variability and quality risk.
Focus 3

Relevance

  • Increase contribution to the group’s highest-value varietal mix.
  • Maintain relevance within the group.
  • Concentrate acreage in the best available genetics.
03 | First Decision

Sunrise to Gold is the value-creating move

The replacement of Sunrise with Gold is not the main debate. Gold is superior from a quality, efficiency, contribution and strategic relevance perspective.

Quality advantage

  • Higher firmness.
  • Larger fruit size.
  • Higher jumbo share.
  • More consistent postharvest performance.

Efficiency advantage

  • Higher harvest productivity.
  • Lower cost per kg.
  • Better labor economics.
  • Greater operational predictability.

Strategic relevance

  • Gold is currently the highest-value variety in the portfolio.
  • Increasing Gold acreage strengthens our contribution to the group.
  • The objective is relevance, not exclusivity.
Conclusion of the first decision: replacing Sunrise with Gold is the correct strategic direction. The remaining question is how to implement Gold.

06 | Second Decision

How should Gold be implemented?

Once Gold is selected, the central discussion becomes implementation. Family Tree prefers containers. Our position is that higher density in soil is more efficient, more profitable, faster and available now.

Strategy Density Yield Curve CAPEX / ha Operational View
Base Gold Scenario 4,915 plants/ha Longer ~USD 23,000 Current design adapted to Gold.
Higher Density in Soil 6,265 plants/ha Shorter / lower kg gap ~USD 27,600 Preferred
Higher Density in Containers 6,265 plants/ha Shorter ~USD 105,000 High CAPEX
Containers require almost 4x the investment per hectare. Any additional yield benefit must be large, validated and sufficient to compensate for the incremental capital and complexity.

08 | What We Know / What We Do Not Know

Certainty should drive the implementation decision

Known

What we know

  • Gold performs better than Sunrise.
  • Gold supports increased density in soil.
  • The soil-based model is already operationally validated.
  • The main value creation comes from the varietal replacement itself.
  • The soil alternative has materially lower CAPEX.
Unvalidated

What we do not know

  • The exact incremental yield advantage of containers.
  • Whether the container benefit compensates nearly 4x CAPEX.
  • Whether higher complexity affects execution, timing or standardization.
  • Whether container-based performance is sufficiently validated under our conditions.
09 | Proposal

Replace Sunrise with Gold using higher density in soil

Proposed alternative: Gold at 6,265 plants per hectare in soil.

Captures Gold value

Delivers the strategic, quality and contribution benefits of the varietal change.

Preserves operating model

Maintains the current system and avoids unnecessary operational disruption.

Improves capital discipline

Requires approximately one quarter of the CAPEX versus containers.

10 | Conclusions

Recommended path

1. Sunrise to Gold is the correct move

It improves quality, efficiency, contribution and relevance. The decision is consistent with the strategic priorities of Family Farms Perú.

2. Soil higher density is the preferred implementation

It captures the majority of expected benefits with lower CAPEX, lower complexity, faster execution and a validated operating model.

Final recommendation: replace Sunrise with Gold in Stage 2, increasing density to 6,265 plants per hectare in soil. This approach delivers higher productivity, stronger contribution, lower capital intensity and lower execution risk.
