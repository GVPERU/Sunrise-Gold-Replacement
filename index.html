Why the plan was reassessed

The review was not intended to reopen the decision indefinitely, but to optimize the replacement design

The original replacement plan contemplated 104 ha at 4,915 plants/ha. Once Gold was evaluated, management identified the opportunity to increase density and improve the economics of the block.

Original premise

Replace the Sunrise block under the original density assumption.
Plant supply was already ordered for 104 ha at 4,915 plants/ha.
The initial execution plan was therefore linked to the existing plant order.

Management reassessment

Gold allows a higher-density soil design.
The value lever is the combination of Gold + density.
Increasing density reduces the number of hectares replaced in the first tranche, but improves the economic design.

The replacement strategy was reassessed to maximize economic value per hectare while preserving execution certainty.

Why pots were not selected

The pot-based alternatives overcapitalize the solution

Pots remain a valid production system to evaluate in other contexts, but they are not financially justified for this specific replacement.

High-density pots

Requires ~US$10.9MM of CAPEX.
Reaches the same stabilized EBITDA as high-density soil under the model.
Produces lower NPV and longer payback.
Adds operational complexity and standardization risk.

New field in pots

Delays the capture of value from replacing Sunrise.
Requires a much larger capital commitment.
Keeps the current block exposed to a decision that already has a stronger answer.
Creates an unnecessary future decision point.

The value lever is not the pot system. The value lever is replacing Sunrise with Gold and increasing density in a capital-efficient way.

Alternatives not selected

Final decision

Proceed with a two-tranche Sunrise-to-Gold replacement under a high-density soil system

This path maximizes value, preserves execution certainty and avoids unnecessary capital intensity.

Decision taken

Management will proceed with Eureka Gold in soil at 6,265 plants/ha. The first tranche will replace approximately 81.6 ha using the plant supply already committed. The remaining 22.4 ha of Sunrise will remain productive and be replaced in the following cycle.
